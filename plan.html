What this looks like in practice

Your client Sarah is a CFO engagement. Every Tuesday she emails her account lead asking for a status update. Two people stop what they're doing to pull together a reply. They check Slack, dig through a shared drive, draft something that sounds confident, and send it. Thirty minutes gone. Multiply that by every active engagement across every practice line.

Now picture this: Sarah stops emailing. Not because she's unhappy. Because she opened a portal, saw her KPIs were green, saw the next deliverable was on track, and went back to running her company. Her account lead spent that thirty minutes on actual client work instead of assembling a status report.

Meanwhile, your ops team just closed a new deal. Three weeks ago, that meant two weeks of manual handoffs before the first consultant could start. Matching, quoting, SOW assembly, staffing requests, onboarding packets. All by hand, all across HubSpot and Firebase and email. Yesterday, the same process took four hours. The consultant started Monday instead of mid-April.

The Real Challenge

Six acquisitions. Six sets of everything.

In the last 14 months, Amplēo acquired Peisner Johnson, Stage Marketing, Peak Business Valuation, Avalaunch Media, AVL Growth Partners, and CMO Zen. Each brought expertise, clients, and proven delivery. Each also brought its own CRM data, its own file systems, its own processes, its own way of quoting, onboarding, and reporting.

Right now, they're six companies that share a brand. Different systems. Different workflows. Different definitions of what a client engagement looks like. The strategic value of those acquisitions compounds only when the data flows and the processes connect.

That's the lab's primary job: build the connective tissue that turns six acquisitions into one platform. Every tool in this plan serves that integration. The portal gives every practice line, acquired or organic, one way to show clients the work. Quote-to-onboard automation gives every practice line one way to sell, staff, and start. AutoResearch gives every consultant, regardless of which company they came from, the same intelligence standard. Marketing productization packages what Stage, Avalaunch, and CMO Zen built into something the whole firm can sell.

Unity invested for exactly this. "Technology and process implementation" isn't abstract. It's making six acquisitions operate as one company.

What Amplēo looks like.

Deals close and consultants start in days, not weeks. The gap between "yes" and "work begins" shrinks from weeks to hours. Every deal that used to sit in a two-week queue of manual handoffs now flows through an automated pipeline: matching, quoting, SOW, staffing, onboarding. That's revenue hitting the books faster on every single deal Amplēo closes, across every practice line.

Practice leads walk into pitches with AI-generated research briefs. Company profiles, leadership changes, financial signals, risk factors. Sources cited, claims verified, ready in minutes instead of hours. They're the best-prepared team in the room, every time.

Standardized packages with clear pricing, onboarding, delivery cadence, and KPI reporting. More deals closed, faster, with less pre-sales effort.

Clients see a portal showing their engagement in real time. Deliverables, KPIs, next actions, communication history. They feel like partners who can see the work happening, not purchasers waiting for the next invoice. Retention goes up because transparency builds trust, and trust compounds into referrals and expansions that no sales process can manufacture.

Unity doesn't see projection spreadsheets. They see adoption dashboards with real usage data from real clients and real employees. Real evidence, not forecasts.
