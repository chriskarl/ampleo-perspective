Amplēo · March 2026 · Prepared by Chris Mayfield & Jon Packer

Amplēo built three decades of trust. Now their clients are asking for something trust alone can't deliver.

Amplēo is one of the most respected fractional executive platforms in the country. Their clients trust them with finance, HR, and marketing. And increasingly, those same clients are asking: can you help us deploy AI? Right now, the honest answer is no. That gap is the conversation.

1
New Service Lines

Amplēo becomes the firm
that delivers AI transformation from inside.

The client is ahead of their competitors on AI. They got there through the same relationship they already had. No new vendor. No separate engagement. The fractional executive they trusted with their finances or their people also led their AI transformation.

Amplēo didn't just retain the relationship. They deepened it. And when the client asks how it happened, the answer is the same thing it's always been: the people we already trusted knew our business. Most companies fail at AI deployment not because of the technology. It fails because the people building it don't know the business. Amplēo's people already do. No other firm can say that.

What Must Be True
01
Clients can point to it
There is a real company, already an Amplēo client, whose finance or HR function runs with AI embedded in it. Not a case study written after the fact. A live example Amplēo's sales team can walk a prospect through. That is what makes the positioning real.
02
A working offering exists
One service line has a defined AI-native offering. It has a name, a scope, a delivery model, and a price. It is not a pilot or an experiment. It is something Amplēo can sell, deliver, and repeat. Most likely finance or HR, where the client demand signal is clearest and the data access is deepest.
03
The build is done
Someone has built the actual AI capability: the logic, the interface, the integration with the client's systems, delivered inside a real engagement. Not designed. Not roadmapped. Built. This is the step most strategy engagements never reach. It is also the step that makes everything else real.
04
A beachhead is chosen
Before the build, one decision is made: which service line, which client type, which specific problem. Not a committee. Not a strategy document with six options. One choice, made deliberately, with the highest-signal evidence available. Everything else follows from that.

Proof
One real client story

The market frame becomes defensible the moment there is one real client who experienced it. Not a pilot. Not an internal demo. A client who can say: Amplēo did this inside our business, and it worked. That story is worth more than any positioning document.

Urgency

Amplēo's clients are already being approached by AI consultants. The window where "trusted advisor who also does AI" is a distinct category won't stay open indefinitely. The firms that move first get credit for being first. In professional services, first-mover trust compounds.

What Must Be True
01
The network operates on a shared standard
All 150+ executives use the same AI operating model, the same tools, the same approach to integrating AI into client work, the same standard for what "AI-enabled" means inside an Amplēo engagement. The inconsistency that exists today is gone. The network is the product.

A unified architecture connects the divisions
Finance, HR, marketing, turnaround: each division is running AI in its own way today. Those experiments have been evaluated, the best systematized, and a shared architecture connects them. The employee handbook Q&A, the Excel modeling, the sales call coaching: no longer isolated pockets. One system.
03
An owner is accountable for it
The transformation has a person behind it. Not a committee, not a council that meets monthly, but someone with authority and accountability. The tech council Joe is already thinking about becomes real when it has an execution owner underneath it.
04
Unity Partners can see the return
The PE investment mandate was explicit: technology investment, systemic improvement, service expansion. There is something concrete to show the board. Not a roadmap. A working capability already generating client results and differentiating Amplēo in the market.
Why Amplēo
Audit
Know what already exists

Before building anything new, understand what is already happening. Which divisions are furthest along. Where the highest-leverage consolidation points are. What the executives who are using AI daily are actually doing, and what is working. The audit turns scattered experimentation into a foundation.

Standard
Define what AI-native means at Amplēo

Not a vendor selection. Not a tool list. A clear definition of what an AI-enabled Amplēo engagement looks like, feels like, and delivers. That standard becomes the thing every new executive is trained on, every existing executive migrates toward, and every client eventually experiences.

Mandate
Someone owns it

The tech council Joe is building is the right instinct. The missing ingredient is a person with a clear mandate to execute. Not advise, not coordinate, but build and ship. The transformation from inconsistent to unified requires someone whose job it is to close that gap, week by week.

Signal
Something real within 30 days

Not a strategy document. Not a pilot still in progress. One working AI capability -- connected to real client data, inside one service line -- that Lincoln can show the board. Two weeks to choose the beachhead. Two weeks to build. That is what changes the narrative from "we are exploring AI" to "we are an AI-native firm."

The firms that define what AI-native professional services looks like will own that category for a decade. Amplēo has the relationships, the trust, and the embedded access to be that firm. The question is only whether they move before someone else does.

The Build Decision

Both journeys require two types of work. The first is strategy: understanding the landscape, defining the beachhead, mapping the path. That work is well served by the firms Amplēo is already in conversation with.

The second is the build. A small team, two or three people, takes the strategy and produces the working thing. The actual AI capability inside the actual service line, integrated with the actual client systems -- HubSpot, NetSuite, ClickUp, the Google Workspace migration. That team needs product thinking to define what the thing should do and AI engineering depth to build it. Those are different skills and they rarely live in the same person.

Most companies that have an AI strategy don't have an AI capability. The decision that separates them is whether someone was accountable for the build. Both types of work are necessary. They are sequential, not competing.

Both transformations start in the same place: one beachhead, one build, one real result. The positioning exists. The market is ready. The right move is to begin.
